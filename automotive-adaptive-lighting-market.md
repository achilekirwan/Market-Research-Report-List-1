<p><p>The automotive adaptive lighting market comprises three primary types: halogen, LED, and xenon. Halogen lights are traditional, cost-effective, and offer decent illumination but consume more energy. LED lights are energy-efficient, have a longer lifespan, and provide superior brightness and flexibility in design, making them popular in modern vehicles. Xenon lights, also known as high-intensity discharge (HID) lights, produce bright, white light and improve visibility significantly, but they are typically more expensive and require specific installations.</p></p>
