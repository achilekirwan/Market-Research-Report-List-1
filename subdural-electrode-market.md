<p><p>Subdural electrodes are neurosurgical devices used to monitor and analyze brain activity, primarily in patients with epilepsy or other neurological disorders. These electrodes are placed beneath the dura mater, the outermost layer of the meninges, allowing for a more precise measurement of cortical activity compared to surface electrodes. Their utilization has become integral in pre-surgical evaluations, facilitating the identification of seizure foci and enabling targeted treatment strategies.</p><p>The Subdural Electrode Market is experiencing significant growth due to a rising prevalence of epilepsy and other neurological conditions that require advanced monitoring techniques.
